Monopole Mobility — Gore Jig Method

Use this tool to translate your Gore-style monopole mobility jig readings into top stiffness K, effective modal mass Meff, and the monopole mobility score—so you can compare guitars on a consistent scale.

Why this matters: Monopole mobility shows how freely the top pumps air at its main resonance. Alongside tap tones and thickness, it helps you avoid tops that are too stiff to respond or too floppy to hold up.

Default dataset: Rick Molloy OM (10/25/2025) — spruce / steel-string, m = 1.02 kg, x = 0.18 mm, fu = 153 Hz
Overview & required measurements

Seal the soundhole, mute the strings, tap the top to find the uncoupled monopole fu, then hang a known mass over the bridge and record deflection x.

Measurements required

  • Uncoupled top monopole frequency fu (Hz)
  • Bridge deflection under test mass x (mm)
  • Test mass m (kg)
  • Guitar type (steel-string, classical, other)

How to use this tool

  1. Seal the soundhole so the box is airtight.
  2. Mute the strings, tap the top, and record the uncoupled top frequency fu with your FFT app.
  3. Mount the mobility jig – hang a known test mass (m) vertically over the bridge and measure deflection (x) in millimeters.
  4. Enter fu, x, and m above. The calculator converts x → meters, computes K, Meff, and mobility.
  5. Review the score and compare to the target ranges.

Measurement Inputs

Enter measurements from a sealed-soundhole, muted-strings tap and jig deflection.

Lower scores mean a stiffer top. Aim for ≈20 on a responsive steel‑string OM, ≈30 for classicals. <15 = very stiff, >40 = very compliant.
Top stiffness K and effective mass Meff can be entered into the 4‑DOF solver to model your guitar’s air/top/back coupling and to generate realistic brace/plate tuning deltas when aiming at specific frequencies.

How this tool works

Based on the Gore & Gilet monopole mobility jig (Vol. 1). The calculator converts your jig measurements into top stiffness at the bridge (K), effective modal mass (Meff), and the standard monopole mobility score.

Force from test mass

The hanging mass sets the applied force.

F = m · g

Top stiffness at the bridge

Deflection (x) is converted to meters.

K = F / x

Effective modal mass

Derived from stiffness and the uncoupled top frequency.

Meff = K / (2π fu)2

Monopole mobility score

Dimensionless index used for build-to-build comparisons.

Mobility score = 1000 / √(K · Meff)
